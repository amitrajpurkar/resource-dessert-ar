# Problem Statement: Resource Dessert - optimizing services reach

Jacksonville is a city of vast geography and diverse neighborhoods. However, essential services are physically or financially out of reach for those who need them most.

Breaking the Cycle
The Inequity Cycle: A lack of "Preventative Assets" (parks and checkups) leads directly to "Poor Outcomes" (Mental Health struggles and lower Life Expectancy).
The Solution: We don't just need more resources; we need the right resources in the right places.
Your Mission: Use AI to bridge the gap between the "Supply" of care and the "Demand" of the community.

Key Questions
Using the provided data, identify specific neighborhoods in Jacksonville that qualifies as a "Resource Desert."
1. What is the biggest mismatch between the residents living there and the essential services currently available to them?
2. How do "Resource Deserts" directly affect the long-term health and well-being of a community?
[Show relationship between a lack of “preventative assets" and the "pooroutcomes“.]
3. How would you use AI to "put the right resources in the right places"? Propose a data-driven solution that bridges the gap for a specific Jacksonville community, ensuring that essential services are no longer "physically or financially out of reach."
